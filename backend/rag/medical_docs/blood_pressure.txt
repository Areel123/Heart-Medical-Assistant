BLOOD PRESSURE OVERVIEW AND MANAGEMENT

This document provides educational information about blood pressure and heart health.
It does not replace medical diagnosis or professional care.

WHAT IS BLOOD PRESSURE

Blood pressure is the force of blood pushing against the walls of the arteries.
It is measured using two numbers:
- Systolic pressure: pressure when the heart beats
- Diastolic pressure: pressure when the heart rests between beats

NORMAL BLOOD PRESSURE RANGES

- Normal: Less than 120/80 mmHg
- Elevated: 120–129 / less than 80 mmHg
- High Blood Pressure (Hypertension Stage 1): 130–139 / 80–89 mmHg
- High Blood Pressure (Hypertension Stage 2): 140/90 mmHg or higher
- Low Blood Pressure (Hypotension): Less than 90/60 mmHg

SYMPTOMS OF HIGH BLOOD PRESSURE

High blood pressure often has no noticeable symptoms.
Possible symptoms may include:
- Headaches
- Dizziness
- Blurred vision
- Chest discomfort
- Nosebleeds (rare)

RISKS OF UNCONTROLLED BLOOD PRESSURE

Uncontrolled blood pressure can lead to:
- Heart attack
- Stroke
- Heart failure
- Kidney damage
- Vision problems

MANAGING BLOOD PRESSURE

Lifestyle measures include:
- Reducing salt intake
- Eating a balanced diet rich in fruits and vegetables
- Regular physical activity
- Maintaining a healthy weight
- Avoiding smoking and excessive alcohol
- Managing stress

Medication may be prescribed by healthcare professionals when lifestyle changes are not sufficient.

WHEN TO SEE A DOCTOR

Consult a healthcare provider if:
- Blood pressure readings remain consistently high
- Symptoms such as chest pain or shortness of breath appear
- There is a family history of heart disease
