EXERCISE GUIDELINES FOR HEART HEALTH

This document provides general fitness guidance for maintaining heart health.
Always consult a healthcare professional before starting a new exercise program.

BENEFITS OF EXERCISE FOR THE HEART

Regular physical activity:
- Strengthens the heart muscle
- Improves blood circulation
- Lowers blood pressure
- Reduces cholesterol levels
- Helps control blood sugar
- Reduces stress and anxiety

RECOMMENDED TYPES OF EXERCISE

1. Aerobic Exercise
Examples include:
- Walking
- Jogging
- Cycling
- Swimming
- Dancing

Recommended duration:
- At least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week

2. Strength Training
Helps improve muscle tone and metabolism.
Examples:
- Light weight lifting
- Resistance bands
- Bodyweight exercises

Recommended frequency:
- 2 days per week

3. Flexibility and Balance
Improves mobility and reduces injury risk.
Examples:
- Stretching
- Yoga
- Tai Chi

SAFETY GUIDELINES

- Start slowly and increase intensity gradually
- Warm up before exercise and cool down afterward
- Stay hydrated
- Stop exercising if chest pain, dizziness, or severe shortness of breath occurs

EXERCISE FOR PEOPLE WITH HEART CONDITIONS

People with known heart conditions should:
- Follow doctor-approved exercise plans
- Avoid sudden intense activity
- Monitor heart rate during exercise

Exercise should be consistent and enjoyable to maintain long-term heart health.
