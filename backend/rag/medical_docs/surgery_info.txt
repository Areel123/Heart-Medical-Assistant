HEART SURGERY INFORMATION

This document provides general educational information about heart-related surgeries.

WHY HEART SURGERY MAY BE NEEDED

Heart surgery may be recommended for:
- Blocked coronary arteries
- Heart valve problems
- Congenital heart defects
- Advanced heart failure

COMMON TYPES OF HEART SURGERY

1. Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting (CABG)
Used to bypass blocked arteries and improve blood flow to the heart.

2. Angioplasty and Stent Placement
A minimally invasive procedure to open narrowed arteries.

3. Heart Valve Repair or Replacement
Repairs or replaces damaged heart valves to improve blood flow.

4. Pacemaker or Implantable Defibrillator
Devices implanted to regulate abnormal heart rhythms.

RISKS AND RECOVERY

Possible risks include:
- Infection
- Bleeding
- Blood clots
- Irregular heart rhythm

Recovery depends on:
- Type of surgery
- Overall health
- Post-surgery care and rehabilitation

POST-SURGERY CARE

- Follow medication instructions carefully
- Attend follow-up appointments
- Gradually return to physical activity
- Maintain a heart-healthy lifestyle

Heart surgery decisions should always be made with a qualified cardiologist or surgeon.
